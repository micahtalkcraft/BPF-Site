### Full Details

Blueberry Funded offers two instant funding accounts that start with a 80% profit share, giving you immediate access to funded capital without any need to work through an evaluation process. Both instant options use a trailing drawdown that moves up as your equity grows, locking in profits while protecting the account.

The key difference between the two instant funding accounts is how risk is managed. The Instant Lite has stricter daily and overall loss limits, while Instant Elite removes the daily loss cap and has a more relaxed overall drawdown, but the initial fees are higher.

### Instant Lite

Instant Lite gives you instant access to funded capital with tighter drawdown limits. You'll work under a 2% daily loss and a 4% total trailing drawdown, which moves up as your balance grows. The setup rewards consistency and helps control exposure from day one.

It's a solid option if you trade shorter timeframes, manage smaller positions, or prefer steady growth with less volatility. The structure keeps risk defined and lets you build results without large balance swings.

### Instant Elite

Instant Elite offers the same direct funding but removes the daily loss limit, giving you a wider 10% total trailing drawdown. You have more room to manage open positions, scale in, or hold trades overnight.

### Other Challenges

If you prefer an evaluation route, Blueberry has four additional challenges. Rapid runs a single 7 day challenge and will suit if you prefer short, high feedback setups. One Step keeps things simple with a static drawdown, while Two Step and Prime offer more structure.

### Trading Fees

Blueberry Funded's trading costs come directly from its broker, Blueberry Markets, using a raw pricing model with fixed commissions.

- **Spreads:** Starting from 0.1 pips on major forex pairs during peak trading hours. Spreads can widen slightly in low-liquidity periods but remain competitive compared to other instant funding firms.
- **Commissions:** $3.50 per side for forex and gold. Indices, commodities, and crypto are commission free. US stock CFDs have a $2 round-turn fee.

This setup provides stable execution costs for both discretionary and automated traders. All pricing is identical across platforms, so conditions stay consistent whether you trade on MT4, MT5, DXTrade, or TradeLocker.

### Trading Platforms

Blueberry Funded supports four of the most popular trading platforms worldwide with MetaTrader 4 (MT4), MetaTrader 5 (MT5), DXTrade, and TradeLocker available.

- **MT4:** Available on evaluation and challenge accounts but not Instant Funding. Designed for manual and EA trading with familiar charting tools and order management.
- **MT5:** Available across all account types including Instant Funding. Supports advanced order types, Depth of Market data, and multi-asset trading such as stocks and crypto.
- **DXTrade:** Web-based platform with TradingView-style charting, ideal for quick execution and clean layout.
- **TradeLocker:** Browser and mobile-based interface focused on fast execution and modern usability.

Instant Funding traders can choose between MT5, DXTrade, or TradeLocker. EA use and weekend trading are permitted, but strategies like latency arbitrage, grid systems, and news scalping remain restricted under standard prop rules.

Profit payouts are available every 14 days through RiseWorks, with a $100 minimum. You keep 80% by default and 90% after consistent results. Accounts that meet performance and withdrawal milestones every three months can scale by 25%, up to a simulated balance of $2 million.

The payout process is fast and predictable, which is a strong point for instant funding traders who rely on frequent withdrawals.

It's also a founding member of The Prop Association, which aims to set industry wide standards on fairness and dispute resolution.

## How Prop Trading Firm Challenges Work

Prop firm challenges are designed to assess traders' skills and commitment by requiring them to achieve set profit targets under strict risk management rules.

These challenges often involve one or two evaluation phases where traders must meet profit objectives, such as 8-10% within a set period while respecting daily and overall drawdown limits. Successfully passing these challenges leads to a funded account, granting traders access to capital while adhering to ongoing risk parameters.
