If you've been active in trading communities online, you've almost certainly seen prop firm ads — promises of $100,000 funded accounts, 90% profit splits, and the chance to trade other people's money. But what actually is a prop firm, how do they work, and are they legitimate?

This guide breaks down everything a beginner needs to know before paying for their first challenge.

## What is a Prop Firm?

A **proprietary trading firm** (or "prop firm") is a company that provides traders with capital to trade financial markets. In exchange, the firm takes a share of any profits the trader generates.

The modern retail prop firm model works slightly differently from traditional institutional prop trading. Rather than hiring traders as employees, today's prop firms use an **evaluation challenge** system to identify profitable traders from the general public and then allocate them a funded trading account.

## How Do Evaluation Challenges Work?

Most prop firms operate a two-stage process:

**1. The Challenge (Evaluation Phase)**

You pay an upfront fee — typically between $50 and $500 depending on account size — to access a simulated trading account. You must hit a profit target (usually 8–10% of account size) within a set number of days, without breaching daily or overall drawdown limits.

**2. The Verification Phase** (on 2-step models)

If you pass the first phase, you move to a second phase with a lower profit target (typically 5%) and the same or stricter drawdown rules. This phase confirms your first performance wasn't a fluke.

**3. The Funded Account**

Once you pass the evaluation, you receive a live or simulated funded account. You trade the same way, but now your profits are real. The firm takes their cut (typically 10–20%) and you keep the rest.

## What Are the Different Account Types?

### 2-Step Challenges
The traditional model with two evaluation phases before funding. Lower fees, stricter rules. Good for disciplined traders.

### 1-Step Challenges
A single evaluation phase before funding. Faster path to a funded account, often with slightly higher fees or tighter drawdown rules.

### Instant Funding
No evaluation required — you get a funded account immediately, but usually with a lower initial profit split that scales up over time, or you pay a higher upfront fee.

### Funded Straight Away (FSA)
Some firms offer accounts that are "funded" from day one, with a profit share model and monthly billing, rather than a one-time challenge fee.

## What Are the Key Rules to Understand?

**Daily Drawdown:** The maximum amount you can lose in a single trading day, expressed as a percentage of your account balance. Breaching this ends your challenge or funded account immediately.

**Maximum Drawdown:** The maximum total loss allowed from your account's peak value. This is your overall risk limit.

**Minimum Trading Days:** Some firms require you to trade for a minimum number of days to prove consistency, even if you hit your profit target quickly.

**Consistency Rule:** Some firms require that no single trade or day represents more than a set percentage of your total profit. This discourages "one lucky trade" strategies.

**News Trading:** Many firms restrict trading during high-impact economic news events (NFP, CPI, Fed decisions). Always check whether this restriction applies.

## How Do I Get Paid?

Once funded, you can request payouts after reaching your firm's minimum payout threshold and waiting any required period (typically 7–14 days from your first profitable trade). Payouts are made via:

- **Bank wire transfer** — standard for larger amounts, may incur fees
- **Cryptocurrency** — fastest, lowest fees, popular with international traders
- **Wise / PayPal** — supported by some firms for smaller amounts

The challenge fee is typically refunded with your first payout at most reputable firms.

## Are Prop Firms Legitimate?

Yes — the majority of established prop firms are legitimate businesses. However, the space has also seen its share of firms that failed to pay out, changed rules retrospectively, or simply disappeared with traders' challenge fees.

**Signs of a legitimate prop firm:**
- High Trustpilot rating (4.0+) with a large number of verified reviews
- Active, engaged social media community
- Transparent, publicly published rules and terms
- Track record of 2+ years
- Clear company registration and team information

**Red flags:**
- No verifiable reviews or very new firm with no community
- Rules that change frequently or are vague about drawdown calculations
- Withdrawal fees or excessive processing delays
- Unrealistic profit targets or no consistency requirements

## How Much Can I Make?

Your earnings depend on your trading performance, account size, and profit split. On a $100,000 account with an 80% profit split:

- A 5% profit month = $5,000 × 80% = **$4,000 to you**
- A 10% profit month = $10,000 × 80% = **$8,000 to you**

Most firms also offer scaling plans — if you consistently hit profit targets, they'll increase your funded account size, sometimes up to $2,000,000 or more.

## Getting Started

1. **Learn to trade first.** A challenge fee is wasted if you don't have a proven, consistent strategy. Paper trade or use a small live account before attempting a challenge.

2. **Start small.** Begin with a smaller account size ($5K–$25K) to learn the rules without risking a large fee.

3. **Read the terms carefully.** Understand exactly how drawdown is calculated, what the consistency rule requires, and when you can request your first payout.
