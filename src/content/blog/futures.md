### Trading Costs and Fees

FXIFY Futures' pricing structure is clear and straightforward, with monthly fees for the evaluation phase and no recurring costs after funding. Unlike other futures prop firms, FXIFY does not charge platform fees, data fees, or per-trade commissions, making it a cost-effective choice for funded trading.

Challenge Fees depend on the account size and plan:

- **Starter Plan:** $89/month for a $50K account, scaling up to $225/month for $150K.
- **Expert Plan:** $149/month for a $50K account, increasing to $349/month for $150K.

Once funded, Starter accounts require a one-time activation fee of $125, while Expert accounts have no activation fee, allowing immediate access to withdrawals.

Profit payouts follow a tiered structure, starting at 60% on the first withdrawal, increasing to 100% after the fifth payout. Before withdrawing, traders must maintain a buffer zone, ensuring account stability. For example, a $50,000 funded account requires at least $500 in profits above the buffer before requesting a payout.

Unlike many futures prop firms that require monthly exchange data fees, FXIFY provides real-time CME data for free. All trading commissions are covered in the evaluation fee, meaning no additional per-trade costs.

Traders can upgrade their profit split by 10% at checkout, increasing the Starter payout from 80% to 90% and Expert from 90% to 100%. Expert traders can also remove daily drawdown limits, providing more flexibility in risk management.

With no hidden fees, no platform costs, and a structured withdrawal model, FXIFY Futures ensures traders keep more of their profits while maintaining a disciplined trading approach.

## What Is A Futures Prop Firm?

A futures prop firm is a proprietary trading company that funds traders to trade futures contracts using the firm's capital. These firms provide access to regulated exchanges like CME and NYMEX, allowing traders to speculate on commodities, indices, and bonds without risking their own money. Traders must pass an evaluation to get funded and follow strict risk management rules. Unlike CFD-based firms, futures prop firms generally offer real market execution rather than simulated trading.

---

## Futures Prop Trading Firms vs. Forex Prop Trading Firms

Futures and forex prop trading firms operate differently, each offering unique advantages and challenges. Futures prop firms provide access to centralized exchanges like CME and NYMEX, while forex firms operate in a decentralized market.

Futures contracts have fixed expiration dates and trade in specific contract sizes, whereas forex allows for flexible lot sizing and 24/5 trading. Leverage also differs, with forex firms often offering higher leverage (up to 1:500) compared to futures, which typically have lower leverage (1:10 – 1:50) but larger contract values.

Futures trading suits structured traders who prefer regulated exchanges, set contract sizes, and diverse assets like commodities, indices, and bonds. Forex is better for traders seeking continuous market access, higher leverage, and smaller trade sizes.

| Feature | Futures Prop Firms | Forex Prop Firms |
|---|---|---|
| Market Type | Centralized (CME, NYMEX, ICE) | Decentralized (OTC) |
| Trading Hours | Limited to exchange hours | 24/5 trading |
| Contract Size | Fixed (e.g., 1 E-mini S&P 500 contract = $50 per point) | Flexible lot sizing |
| Leverage | 1:10 – 1:50 (exchange-regulated) | Up to 1:500 (broker-dependent) |
| Asset Classes | Commodities, indices, bonds, metals | Currencies (majors, minors, exotics) |
| Pricing | Transparent (regulated exchanges) | Variable (broker spreads, liquidity providers) |
| Market Depth | High liquidity but limited order flow visibility | Deep liquidity but no central exchange |

## Risk Management Tools

Risk management is a critical component of success in futures prop trading. Unlike self-funded accounts where you set your own risk parameters, prop firms impose strict drawdown limits, daily loss caps, and trade restrictions to protect their capital.

If you fail to adhere to these rules you may face disqualification, making proper risk management essential for maintaining a funded account.

Futures prop firms typically enforce the following risk controls:

**Daily Loss Limits:** You are restricted to a maximum loss per day, which is often calculated as a percentage of your total account balance (e.g., 4-6% of a $50K account). Exceeding this amount results in account termination.

**Trailing Drawdowns:** A common risk rule in prop trading, trailing drawdowns adjust dynamically as the account grows, ensuring you maintain discipline and avoid reckless losses.

**Max Position Size Limits:** Futures prop firms impose contract size restrictions to prevent excessive leverage usage, limiting the number of contracts you can hold at once.

**Time-Based Trading Restrictions:** Some firms restrict trading during high-volatility news events or require you to close positions before the market closes.

Effective risk management strategies for prop traders include:

- **Starting with Small Positions:** Using micro futures (MES, MNQ, MGC) before scaling up to full-size contracts helps manage early-stage risks.
- **Setting Stop-Loss Orders:** Implementing stop-losses on every trade prevents excessive losses and aligns with firm risk policies.
- **Diversifying Trades:** Avoiding overexposure to a single asset by trading across indices, commodities, and bond futures reduces risk concentration.
- **Respecting Drawdown Limits:** Monitoring account balance and adjusting contract sizes as the account grows helps maintain long-term funding.

By adhering to these principles, you can preserve your funded accounts while steadily growing their capital under a prop firm's structured risk management policies.

### What is the 80% rule in futures trading?

The 80% rule in futures trading is a market pattern where if a price enters a previous trading range and moves 20% into it, there is an 80% chance it will continue to the other side of the range. This rule is commonly used in market profile analysis and applies to futures contracts trading within well-defined price levels. Traders use it to identify potential breakouts or reversals within a trading range.
